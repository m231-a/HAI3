## What is HAI3 (for us)?

For our team, HAI3 represents one variation of the **frontend layer for the Cyberfabric platform**.
It's designed to help us create microfrontends and application providers with essential building blocks‚Äîroutes, pages, screens, forms, and related components‚Äîall with AI assistance.

What makes it distinctive is that it's not just a framework.
It's an **integrated ecosystem** of tools, scripts, workflows, and AI solutions working together.

---

## Why We Use It (Or Plan To)

The value proposition is compelling. HAI3 promises to **increase our development speed**, improve the **final quality** of our applications, and ensure **consistency across multiple frontends and apps**‚Äîsomething that's challenging to achieve manually.

What excites me most is the vision of **tight integration** between human development, AI support, and our company's software development workflows. The idea that a **single-sourced solution** could provide better developer experience and better products is exactly what we need as we scale.

---

## What I Appreciate

I genuinely like the **core concept**: bringing together human expertise, AI capabilities, and established company workflows into one cohesive approach.

The potential for **cross-application consistency** is valuable. When done right, this kind of unified approach should make our lives easier, not harder. And the ambition to create tooling that works seamlessly with AI assistance‚Äîthat's forward-thinking and aligns with where the industry is heading.

---

## What Concerns Me

However, I need to be honest about some **practical challenges** we're experiencing:

**First**, the development process feels **somewhat chaotic** right now. We lack a clear, stable roadmap. As developers, we're hesitant to commit to HAI3 fully because we're anticipating breaking changes almost daily. This creates uncertainty and makes long-term planning difficult.

**Second**, when creating applications from scratch using the current tooling and workflows, we're seeing about **40% success rate initially**. The remaining 60% requires manual fixes or falling back to simpler AI prompts‚Äîoften without the benefit of our specs and company workflows that HAI3 is supposed to integrate with.

**Third**, there's an issue with **contracts and conventions**. The contracts between different development layers aren't firmly established, so different teams are adopting their own approaches. This defeats one of HAI3's main purposes: consistency.

**Finally**, and this is important: HAI3 currently contains a lot of **example code and patterns that we can't actually use in real development**. This creates confusion about what's production-ready and what's just demonstration material.

---

## My Proposals

To address these concerns, I'd like to suggest a few concrete steps:

**One**: Let's invest more time in **planning and design** before implementation. Moving fast is good, but moving in the right direction is better.

**Two**: Let's **involve practicing developers** in design discussions earlier and more often. We're the ones who'll live with these decisions daily, and we can identify practical issues before they become technical debt.

**Three**: Let's establish a **clear roadmap with milestones** and commit to following it. Stability matters for adoption. Developers need to trust that what they build today won't be obsolete next week.

**Four**: Let's have an explicit discussion about **how and when HAI3 integrates into our Cyberfabric workflows**. Right now, that integration story isn't clear, and it's preventing us from making confident architectural decisions.

---

## Conclusion

I believe in HAI3's potential. The vision is sound. But to realize that vision, we need **more structure, better communication with developers, and clearer integration plans**.

With those improvements, HAI3 could become exactly what it's meant to be: a powerful, consistent, AI-enhanced frontend solution for our entire platform.

Thank you.
